2017-2018: Kevin Durant Era

On July 4th, 2016, Kevin Durant made the infamous decision to leave the Oklahoma City Thunder and sign with the Golden State Warriors. This Golden State Warriors team was widely considered a superteam, and many NBA fans though this addition was overkill, even though they fell short in the title game the year prior. Some speculated whether there would be a long adjusting period for the team, with Stephen Curry inevitably having less usage than the years prior. However, the team dominated the league immediately, with Curry and Durant averaging around 25 points per game, losing only fifteen games in the regular sesaon. The momentum carried on into the playoffs, as they lost only 1 game, and Kevin Durant won his first Finals MVP.

Although they did not win as many games in the regular season as the year prior, Klay Thompson saw an increase in scoring output, dropping 20 points per game to help carry some of the offensive load. After a close conference finals against the Houston Rockets, scraping by in a Game 7, they easily swept the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Finals, winning back to back titles and Kevin Durant earned his second Finals MVP.

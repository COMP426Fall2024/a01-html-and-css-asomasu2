2022: The Comeback

After their championship in 2018, they made the finals the year after, but lost to the Toronto Raptors. The team went through a number of changes in between this season and the 2021-2022 season, leading to two dissapointing sesaons leading up to it.


Date Event
June 13, 2019 Klay Thompson tears his ACL in his left knee in game 6 of the Finals, causing him to miss the two following seasons.
July 7, 2019 Kevin Durant gets moved to the Brookly Nets in a sign-and-trade deal, ending his legendary but controversial run with the Golden State Warriors.
October 20, 2019 Stephen Curry suffers a broken hand injury, causing him to only play in 5 games that season.

With many NBA analysts questioning whether the Warriors could contend for another title in the near future, they made two key additions before the beginning of the season. They signed Andrew Wiggins, and drafted Jordan Poole, two contributors that could make up for the loss of Kevin Durant. The team was perfectly balanced, with Stephen Curry once again leading the charge, just like 2015 season, and Klay Thompson looking like his old self. With a finish of 3rd in the Western Conference, the Warriors cruised to the playoffs, not losing more than 2 games in any series. They beat a young but tough Boston Celtics team in the NBA Finals to get their fourth and last championship in the current Warriors dynasty.
